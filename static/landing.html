How The AI Works

9 AI Agents Working Together

OncoAI uses a Stanford Medicine-style multi-agent orchestrator. Specialized AI agents analyze patient data simultaneously, communicate findings to each other, and produce a unified clinical intelligence report.

Intelligent Agent Pipeline

Each patient case flows through a coordinated pipeline of specialized AI agents. The orchestrator analyzes the patient's data — cancer type, stage, lab results, imaging, pathology — and automatically selects which agents to invoke.

Smart Routing

Automatically detects which agents are needed based on available patient data — no manual selection required.

Agent Communication

Agents share findings with each other — the patient profile informs the trial matcher, risk scores feed into the TB briefing.

Guideline Compliance

Every recommendation is validated against NCCN and WHO head & neck cancer guidelines before reaching the tumor board.

Safety Alerts

Critical lab values, drug interactions, allergies, and care gaps are flagged in real-time — before they become emergencies.

The Challenge

Why Head & Neck Cancer Care Needs Innovation

At institutions like Muhimbili National Hospital and ORCI, cancer care faces systemic challenges that delay treatment and reduce outcomes.

Late-Stage Presentation

Most patients arrive with Stage III–IV disease due to delays in referral, diagnosis, and financial barriers to care.

Fragmented Care Pathways

Patient records are scattered across facilities. No centralized system tracks workup completion or tumor board readiness.

Limited Specialist Access

Pathologists and radiologists are scarce. Multidisciplinary coordination is ad-hoc, with no structured discussion or follow-up.
